Incident Report — Synthetic Case File SF-2019-014
Facility: Chemical Processing Unit, Zone 4
Date: March 2019 (illustrative synthetic case, for demo purposes)

Summary: A gas leak developed in a storage area while a maintenance permit
was active for unrelated valve replacement work in the same zone. Gas
sensors recorded a steady rise in ambient concentration over roughly 40
minutes, but the maintenance crew was not alerted because the permit
system and the gas monitoring system were never cross-checked against
each other. A worker entered the zone to complete the valve replacement
and was exposed to fumes before evacuation procedures began.

Root cause: No mechanism existed to flag that an active permit and rising
sensor readings in the same zone represented a compound risk. Each system
worked correctly on its own, but nothing connected them.

Lesson: Any active permit-to-work in a zone with rising gas concentration
should trigger an automatic hold on entry, regardless of whether the gas
level has yet crossed the standalone alarm threshold.
